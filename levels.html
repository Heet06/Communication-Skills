Levels of communication

Communication between humans takes place at the following levels.

1.Extrapersonal:

This level of communication takes place between humans and other species.

Examples:

Your cat coming near you or when you pet them.

2.Intrapersonal:

This level of communication takes place within an individual. Self-motivation is an excellent example of intrapersonal communication.

Examples:

While preparing for an examination, we reassure ourselves with words like 'you can do it' and 'you are prepared'.

3.Interpersonal:

This level of communication occurs between human beings.In this level of communication, the roles of sender and receiver change constantly. Sharing information, exchanging ideas and negotiating are all examples of interpersonal communication

4.Organisational:

This level of communication takes place between and within organisations. In this type of communication, networks of communications may very.

5.Mass:

This is when the sender has to reach out to a large mass of people. Examples of this type of communication are: announcements, information sent out through pamphlets, advertisements, etc.. In this level, the sender selects an appropriate medium such as newspapers, books, magazines, etc. to reach to the masses.
